About Me

I've always designed
for how people move.

My name is Rutuja Kulgod. For nearly a decade I designed the built environment — landscape architecture and urban infrastructure projects across Singapore and India, working with agencies like NParks, LTA, HDB, and PUB at firms including DP Architects and Henning Larsen.

That work shaped everything I do now. Site analysis, stakeholder negotiation, multi-agency coordination, spatial reasoning, post-occupancy evaluation — these aren't just architecture skills. They're the foundation of rigorous, human-scale UX thinking. I learned to design for how people actually inhabit spaces long before I designed interfaces.

In 2023 I completed a UX Design Immersive at General Assembly Singapore and pivoted fully into digital product design. The transition felt inevitable: I was already doing user research. I was already managing complex delivery timelines. I was already thinking in systems. The medium changed. The discipline didn't.

Today I lead end-to-end UX strategy across enterprise SaaS, core banking, AEC industry tools, and digital media at Utterwise Technologies — and I bring the same precision to digital products that I once brought to a site plan. I'm based in Singapore (PR).

The challenge

Problem statement

ESD engineers managing sustainability data were overwhelmed by fragmented tools: energy reports in Excel, emissions data in PDFs, water usage in separate SCADA systems. Critical anomalies were spotted too late — or not at all.

The opportunity

Unify 7 disparate data sources into a single, role-appropriate dashboard that enables management to track ESG goals and operators to respond to real-time anomalies with confidence and speed.

Phase 1
Multi-tenant recruitment platform — hiring setup & candidate sourcing
5 Roles
Role-based workflows from Platform Admin to Sourcing Recruiter
Problem Space

Challenges

Hiring processes are fragmented across tools — ATS systems, spreadsheets, and emails. Workflows lack standardisation, are difficult to scale across teams and vendors, and provide poor visibility across candidates and hiring stages.

Goal

Design a scalable, role-based platform to streamline hiring setup and candidate sourcing — unifying multi-tenant management, complex org hierarchies, and multi-channel sourcing into one coherent system.

Current state — fragmented toolchain
Users & Roles

Key personas — derived from SRS role definitions. Each role has different permissions and visibility, requiring an adaptive UI that responds to context rather than showing everything to everyone.

Platform Admin

Manages all tenants & global platform settings

Tenant Admin

Manages users & org structure within a tenant

Biz Admin

Manages groups, cost centres & evaluation forms

Rec. Manager

Creates & configures hiring projects

Sourcing Recruiter

Sources & submits candidates to open projects

Design challenge: Each role has different permissions and visibility. The UI must adapt — showing the right capabilities at the right time — without requiring users to understand what they cannot do. Progressive disclosure and permission-based rendering were non-negotiable from day one.
UX Strategy

Approach

Used progressive disclosure, modular design, and reusable patterns to manage complexity. A shared component system was built and then permission-filtered at the component level — keeping the design system lean and the UX consistent across all 5 roles.

IA Foundation

Information architecture was settled before any screen was designed. Every module maps to a clear system level (platform vs tenant), a primary owner role, and a set of downstream dependencies — preventing scope creep and conflicting permissions mid-design.

System hierarchy — platform to tenant to modules
Consistency

Tables, filters, and side panels reused across all modules

Scalability

Designed for N tenants — patterns hold at enterprise scale

System Status

Every state visible — tenant, user, candidate, hiring stage

Error Prevention

Validation, confirmations, duplicate detection upstream

Reflection

Learnings

Designing for multi-tenant systems requires strong system thinking and role-based UX. The biggest challenge was not any individual screen — it was ensuring every pattern decision (tables, side panels, status indicators) held its meaning consistently across 5 role contexts. Ambiguity at the system level creates confusion at the interaction level.

Next Steps

Enhance visual design fidelity across the full component library. Introduce AI-assisted features — resume parsing, smart candidate matching — as a Phase 2 layer. Optimise recruiter workflows for mobile, since sourcing increasingly happens off-desk. Validate all flows through moderated usability testing with real recruiters.

Key takeaway: RBAC heavily influences UX decisions at every level — it is not a feature, it is a foundational constraint that shapes navigation, component rendering, and system feedback from day one. Breaking complex workflows into guided steps does not just improve usability — it builds trust in a high-stakes system where errors have real downstream consequences.
