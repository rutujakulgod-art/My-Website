01 — The Challenge

A clarity problem,
not a data problem

ESD engineers had access to data — but it was scattered across Excel files, PDFs, and isolated SCADA systems. The real challenge was designing one system that speaks differently to three distinct audiences.

Problem statement

The primary task was to research how we can design a dashboard to track, visualise, and manage sustainability-related metrics for a client — customised to reflect key Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) indicators and support decision-making aimed at achieving sustainability goals.

The opportunity

Unify 7 disparate data sources — energy reports in Excel, emissions in PDFs, water usage in separate SCADA systems — into a single, role-appropriate platform. Critical anomalies were being spotted too late, or not at all. A 4.2-hour average delay from incident detection to corrective action was costing real resources.

01

Informed Decision-Making

Centralise critical data for actionable insights — replacing 2-day manual compilation before every board meeting.

02

Accountability

Make sustainability performance transparent for all stakeholders — from board to plant floor operator.

03

Goal Tracking

Track progress toward long-term sustainability targets with a single ESG score and progress visualisation.

04

Engagement

Encourage employees and operators to participate actively in sustainability efforts through visible, real-time feedback.

05

Regulatory Compliance

Help the organisation meet reporting obligations — LEED, Green Mark, WELL — with automated ESG exports.

06

Anomaly Response

Reduce the average 4.2-hour delay from incident detection to corrective action through smart, prioritised alerting.

User Research & Analysis

After understanding various types of users and their needs, it was concluded that there will be 3 types of dashboards to cater for distinct users and their requirements.

📊
Management Dashboard
Designed for building managers and sustainability directors who need at-a-glance current status and long-term ESG performance. Read-only. Exportable for board reporting.
  • Water consumption & recycling rates
  • Energy usage by source (renewable/non-renewable)
  • Carbon footprint tracking
  • Overall ESG performance vs. targets
  • Suggestions and tactics based on trends
⚙️
Operational Dashboard
Provides detailed real-time KPI overview for operators. Fully editable — users can log actions, raise tickets, and update status. Designed for shift-by-shift use.
  • Live sensor feeds with sparkline trends
  • Smart alert prioritisation (critical/warning/info)
  • Root cause context — pattern-matched anomaly history
  • Shift checklist and handover notes
  • Incident logging and escalation workflows
🌱
Public-Facing Dashboard
Provides overall building performance metrics at a glance for tenants, visitors, and the public. Non-editable, with a feedback mechanism for reported issues.
  • High-level sustainability scorecard
  • Energy and water consumption simplified
  • Green certifications (LEED, Green Mark, WELL)
  • Community participation metrics
  • Issue feedback channel

04 — Design Approach

Design principles

Four principles drove every decision — from information hierarchy to alert severity taxonomy.

01
Signal over noise
Only surfaces data that requires attention. Everything else stays accessible — not prominent. Operators suffered from alert fatigue; the design uses smart severity tiers (critical / warning / info / resolved) to restore trust in alerts.
02
Role-appropriate depth
Management sees trends and ESG scores. Operators see real-time sensor data and shift checklists. Same underlying data, two entirely different lenses. Same platform, zero cognitive mismatch.
03
Actionable context
Every metric links to a recommended action, historical benchmark, or escalation path. Anomaly cards include pattern-matched history ("matches Jan 2026 compressor fault") so operators can act without needing to dig.
04
Progressive disclosure
Overview → drill-down → raw data. Never overwhelm on first load. The hierarchy supports building → campus → masterplan levels of data granularity without cluttering the primary view.

Key Features

📈
Interactive Visuals
Charts, sparklines, and trend maps that allow users to explore data intuitively — from a summary KPI to raw sensor readings.
⚡
Real-Time Updates
Auto-refreshes every 30 seconds to reflect current conditions. Live sensor readings with sparkline trails showing trajectory, not just snapshot values.
👁
Customisable Views
Different stakeholders focus on metrics relevant to their roles. Role switching preserves context; data depth scales to the user's access level.
🏆
Benchmarking
Compares performance against LEED, Green Mark, and WELL industry standards — plus past data and internal targets — in a single progress view.
🔮
Predictive Analytics
Uses AI and ML to forecast energy trends and identify anomaly patterns. Operators receive pattern-matched context — not just raw alerts.
🏗
Hierarchy Tracking
Enables tracking at building, campus, and masterplan scale. Managers can drill into any level; operators stay scoped to their facility.

What the design solved

<5m

What worked well
  • ✓Deep research before design prevented costly assumptions about what data to surface
  • ✓Three-view strategy (Management, Operational, Public) gave each user group exactly what they needed
  • ✓Pattern-matched anomaly context restored trust in alerts and reduced fatigue significantly
  • ✓KPI hierarchy (building → campus → masterplan) enabled scalable data architecture
